History & Key Events (2001 – 2026)

Kiribati has faced many challenges and achievements over the past 25 years. From 2001 to 2026, the nation has focused on:

  • Climate change adaptation and advocacy at the UN
  • Infrastructure development in Tarawa and outer islands
  • Education and healthcare improvements
  • Presidential elections and democratic reforms
  • Preservation of traditional culture and Maneaba systems

Climate Change & Environment

Kiribati is at the frontline of climate change. Rising sea levels, coastal erosion, and ocean acidification threaten the livelihood of its citizens. The government and international organizations are working on relocation planning, sustainable fishing, and renewable energy projects.

High Schools in Kiribati

Kiribati has a range of senior secondary schools located across its islands, serving students from forms 1–7 (secondary education). Many are government schools, while others are operated by church organizations.

Culture & Traditions

Kiribati culture emphasizes respect, unity, and sharing. The Maneaba is central to social life. Traditional skills include weaving, canoe building, fishing, and storytelling.

Government & Economy

Kiribati is a democratic republic led by Te Beretitenti. The government focuses on sustainable development, education, health, and climate resilience. Economy relies on fishing, copra production, and remittances.
